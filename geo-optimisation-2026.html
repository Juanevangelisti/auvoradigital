AI & GEO

What is GEO and Why Your Business Needs It in 2026

April 2026 · 5 min read
JE
Juan Evangelisti
Digital Marketing Specialist · Auvora Digital

If you've been focused on SEO for the past few years, you're already ahead of most small businesses. But in 2026, there's a new game in town — and it's changing the way people find businesses online.

It's called GEO: Generative Engine Optimisation. And if you haven't heard of it yet, now is the perfect time to start paying attention.

What is GEO?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimisation — the practice of optimising your online presence so that AI tools like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Gemini recommend your business when users ask relevant questions.

Think about it. When was the last time you asked ChatGPT for a recommendation? More and more people are doing exactly that:

  • "What's the best digital marketing agency in Sunshine Coast?"
  • "Which local coffee roasters in Brisbane have good reviews?"
  • "Find me a holiday apartment in Noosa under $300 a night."

Traditional SEO helps you rank on Google. GEO helps you get mentioned by AI — and that's a completely different challenge.

Key insight: Studies show that over 30% of internet users now regularly use AI tools for search and recommendations — a number that's growing rapidly in 2026.

How is GEO Different from Traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO GEO
Rank on Google search results Get cited by AI tools
Focus on keywords & backlinks Focus on authority & structured content
Blue link in search results Mentioned in AI-generated answers
User clicks through to your site AI recommends your business directly
Measured in rankings Measured in AI mentions & citations

The good news? GEO and SEO work together. Many of the fundamentals overlap — quality content, authority signals, structured data. But GEO requires some additional strategies that most businesses haven't implemented yet.

Why Does Your Business Need GEO in 2026?

1. AI search is growing fast

Google has integrated AI Overviews into its search results. Perplexity is gaining millions of users. ChatGPT is now used by over 100 million people. Your potential customers are already using these tools — the question is whether your business shows up when they ask.

2. Early movers win

Most Australian small businesses haven't started thinking about GEO yet. That means if you act now, you have a significant advantage over your competitors. Just like early SEO adopters in the 2000s dominated their niches for years, early GEO adopters will dominate AI search results in the years ahead.

3. AI recommendations carry massive trust

When a friend recommends a business, you trust it. When an AI tool recommends a business in response to a specific question, users treat it with similar trust. An AI mention can be more valuable than a paid ad because it feels organic and trustworthy.

4. It's not just ChatGPT

GEO covers a range of AI platforms: Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Copilot and more. Each one is increasingly being used for local and business searches — and each one can recommend your business if your online presence is optimised correctly.

How to Start with GEO

Here are the key foundations of a solid GEO strategy for any business:

  • Create authoritative content — AI tools favour businesses that publish clear, expert-level content in their niche
  • Get cited online — mentions in directories, reviews, and media help AI tools identify your business as relevant
  • Use structured data (Schema markup) — helps AI tools understand exactly what your business does, where you are, and who you serve
  • Build a strong Google Business Profile — Google's AI pulls heavily from GMB data for local recommendations
  • Collect and maintain reviews — AI tools use sentiment and review data to assess business quality
  • Be consistent across all platforms — your name, address, phone number and description should match everywhere online

Is GEO Right for Your Business?

Short answer: yes, for almost every business. But it's especially valuable if you're in a service-based industry, hospitality, retail, or any business where people search for recommendations before buying.

If you're a restaurant in Noosa, a resort on the Sunshine Coast, or a tradie in Brisbane — people are asking AI tools about businesses like yours right now. The question is: are you showing up?

The bottom line: GEO is not a replacement for SEO — it's the next layer. Businesses that combine solid SEO foundations with a GEO strategy will have a significant competitive advantage in 2026 and beyond.

Getting Started

If you're not sure where to begin, the best first step is an AI visibility audit — checking whether your business currently appears in responses from major AI tools, and identifying the gaps in your online presence that are holding you back.
